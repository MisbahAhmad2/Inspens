Getting Started

How do I add my first transaction?

To add your first transaction, click on either "Add Income" or "Add Expense" button on your dashboard. Fill in the required details like amount, category, date, and any notes you want to include. Then click "Save" to record your transaction.

Can I import data from other finance apps?

Currently, we support CSV imports from most major financial apps. Go to Settings > Import Data and follow the instructions to upload your CSV file. We'll guide you through matching columns to our system.

How do I set up budget categories?

Navigate to Settings > Categories where you can add, edit, or remove categories. You can create custom categories that match your spending habits. Default categories are provided but can be modified.

Account Management

How do I change my password?

Go to your Profile page and click on "Change Password". You'll need to enter your current password and then your new password twice for confirmation. Passwords must be at least 8 characters long.

Can I export my financial data?

Yes, you can export all your transactions to CSV format. Go to Reports > Export Data. You can choose date ranges and specific transaction types to include in your export.

How do I delete my account?

Account deletion is available in your Profile settings under "Account Preferences". Be aware this will permanently erase all your data. We recommend exporting your data first if you might need it later.

Budgeting Tips

The 50/30/20 Rule

Allocate 50% of income to needs, 30% to wants, and 20% to savings/debt repayment. This simple framework helps balance spending while building financial security.

Regular Reviews

Schedule weekly check-ins to review transactions and monthly sessions to evaluate your budget. Regular monitoring helps catch issues early and adjust spending habits.

Category Optimization

Use our category breakdown to identify spending patterns. Look for categories where you consistently overspend and set realistic limits or find alternatives.

Goal Setting

Set specific financial goals (emergency fund, vacation, debt payoff) and track progress in our Goals section. Visualizing progress motivates better money habits.

Trend Analysis

Use our monthly/yearly comparison tools to spot seasonal spending patterns and plan ahead for predictable expenses like holidays or annual subscriptions.

Alerts & Reminders

Set up notifications for when you approach budget limits in any category. These gentle nudges help prevent overspending before it happens.
